R
Policy Recommendations for Urban Indigenous Self-Determination
1. Direct Federal FundingEstablish dedicated federal line items for urban Indian CBOs without state/local pass-through.
2. Multi-Year Unrestricted Grants3–5 year grants that place decision-making authority with the community.
3. Reform Funding FormulasInclude urban Indians in all federal Native program eligibility and funding calculations.
4. Invest in CapacityFund technical assistance, financial systems, and strategic planning for small CBOs.
5. Protect Safety NetMaintain WIOA, CDBG, TANF, SNAP, LIHEAP — the financial foundation of urban Indian services.
6. Fund Culture DirectlyCreate a dedicated federal stream for language revitalization and cultural preservation.
7. Disaggregate DataMandate urban AI/AN data collection across all federal agencies.
8. Modernize ReportingUpdate IRS 990 to capture funding sources and program-level data in machine-readable format.
⚠
Safety Net at Risk

A 10% reduction in government grants would eliminate $9.1 million from non-health organizations — the equivalent of wiping out 2 average-sized organizations entirely. Federal programs at risk: WIOA, CDBG, ESG, SAMHSA block grants, IHS Urban Indian Health, Tribal TANF, LIHEAP.
